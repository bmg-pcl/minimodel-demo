Select a viewer mode.

Recommended

Model Viewer + AR

Google Model Viewer with property sidebar and greyscale option. Tap the AR button to place the model on a real-world surface — no marker needed.

Requires: iOS or Android, modern browser
WebXR

WebXR AR Viewer

Full WebXR immersive AR session using surface hit-test. Includes a property sidebar, model nudge controls, and a splash screen on launch.

Requires: Android + Chrome + ARCore, HTTPS
Marker AR

Marker-Based AR

AR.js viewer anchored to a physical marker pattern. Broader browser compatibility than WebXR — no ARCore required, but a printed marker is needed.

Requires: Custom marker pattern (custom-marker.patt)
Cardboard

Google Cardboard

Stereoscopic split-screen viewer with gyroscope head tracking. Drop your phone into a Cardboard headset for an immersive 3D experience — no headset pairing required.

Requires: Phone with gyroscope, Cardboard-style headset
WebXR VR

VR Headset Viewer

Full WebXR immersive-vr session for standalone and tethered headsets. Orbit controls on desktop; controller rays in VR. Supports Meta Quest, Pico, and similar devices.

Requires: WebXR-capable VR headset or desktop browser, HTTPS
HoloLens

HoloLens Viewer

Holographic display via HoloJS and Three.js. Open in the HoloLens Edge browser or deploy through the HoloJS runtime.

Requires: Microsoft HoloLens + HoloJS
